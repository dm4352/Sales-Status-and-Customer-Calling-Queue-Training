üìã Customer Calling Queue Management

The Customer Calling Queue is your To-Do List for customer contacts. It fills up when:

  • Material becomes available (Call Available/Complete)
  • Specified date arrives (Call On Date)
  • Delivery promises are at risk (Trouble Queue)

Understanding "Queue" vs "To-Do List"

Joe's explanation: "Queue" isn't really American vernacular - it's a British term meaning "line" (like in The Who's song "Magic Bus" - "Every day I get on the queue"). In computers, a queue should be "first in, first out" - orderly like a bus line.

But in Eclipse, we've adapted the word queue to really mean To-Do List. You can reshuffle, resort, and reprioritize - it's not truly first-in-first-out.

Queue Types Available:
  • New Items: Customers to call because material is now available OR the specified call date has arrived
  • Trouble Queue: Customers to call when delivery promises are at risk (proactive communication)
  • Branch-Specific Queues: Filter by your specific location
  • Customer-Specific Widgets: Create micro-queues for your 5 favorite customers
Pro Tip: You can create multiple queue widgets on your desktop with pre-answered parameters (branch, customer, writer) so you see exact counts and get flash notifications when counts change. Reach out to BDL by creating a ticket to learn more.

Joe's Philosophy on Customer Contact

"Every contact with a customer is good contact, even when there's friction. It's better to proactively tell your customer today 'Hey Johnny, I told you I'd have that stuff Monday, but I just found out my vendor won't deliver until Tuesday' than to receive a phone call Monday asking 'Where's my material?'"

The Trouble Queue helps you make these proactive calls when delivery dates are at risk.

üìö Quick Reference Guide

When to Use Each Status:

  • Pick Up Now: Customer waiting, everything in stock
  • Ship Complete: Job requires all materials together
  • Ship Available: Customer wants partial shipments
  • Ship On Date: Specific delivery timing required
  • Call Complete: Need to confirm before shipping complete order

Queue Management Tips:

  • Work your calling queue daily
  • Log detailed notes when calling customers
  • Watch for high kick counts (>10 is suspicious)
  • Use Trouble Queue to proactively communicate delays
  • Consider converting old orders back to bids
